Set up in 15 minutes

Set up once, forget about it.

1

Create a free Enable Banking account

Register at enablebanking.com and create an application. This is the regulated open banking provider that gives Klartion read-only access to your bank. Free, takes 5 minutes.

2

Run docker compose up -d

Download the docker-compose.yml and start Klartion. Works on any machine with Docker — Linux, Mac, Raspberry Pi, NAS, a cheap VPS.

3

Follow the browser-based setup wizard

Open Klartion in your browser. The wizard walks you through entering your licence key, connecting your Notion database, uploading your Enable Banking key, and setting up notifications.

4

Connect your bank

Search for your bank, approve read-only access, and choose a sync schedule. Transactions flow into your Notion database daily, automatically.

FAQ

Common questions.

Which banks are supported?
Any of the 2,500+ banks across 29 European countries supported by Enable Banking, including Revolut, N26, Monzo, Wise, Millennium BCP, Santander, ING, BNP Paribas, and many more. Personal accounts only.
Do I need to know how to code?
No coding needed. You'll need Docker installed on your machine, but after that the entire setup is done through a browser-based wizard — no terminal commands beyond docker compose up -d.
Is my bank data safe?
Yes. Klartion uses read-only access via Enable Banking's regulated open banking API — it cannot move money or make any changes to your account. Your data goes directly to your machine and never passes through our servers.
What do I need to run Klartion?
A machine that can run Docker (Mac, Linux, Raspberry Pi, NAS, or a small VPS), a free Enable Banking account for open banking access, and a Notion account with a database to sync into. Klartion is lightweight and uses very little memory or CPU.
Can I use it on more than one machine?
Each licence key can be activated on up to 2 machines. If you move to a new machine, deactivate your current installation from within the app and reactivate on the new one.
Is there a hosted version?
Not yet, but it's coming. Join the waitlist above to be notified when it launches.
